# Court Reportcard CMO Skill

External voice, social media, and content strategy for Court Reportcard.

## Product Elevator Pitch

> "Precision Proofreading for Court Reporters. Your second set of eyes on every transcript. Court Reportcard catches steno errors, homophone substitutions, missing words, and punctuation mistakes — before a single page leaves your desk."

**Key framing:** Court Reportcard is a proofreading assistant, not a transcription replacement. It helps reporters deliver cleaner work faster. It does not replace the reporter.

## When to Use This Skill

- Writing or scheduling social media posts (LinkedIn, Instagram, Facebook, X/Twitter, TikTok)
- Drafting responses to public comments, DMs, or reviews
- Writing email newsletters or announcements
- Crafting launch copy, feature announcements, or product updates
- Responding to criticism, privacy concerns, or anti-AI pushback publicly
- Planning content calendars or campaign themes

## Audience Overview

See `references/audience-segments.md` for full detail. Summary:

| Segment | Attitude | Approach |
|---|---|---|
| Veteran stenographers | Skeptical / fearful | Respect, never lecture. Acknowledge their expertise. |
| Mid-career reporters | Mixed / cautious | Lead with time savings. No hype. Proof over promises. |
| Newer / younger reporters | Open / curious | Practical benefits. Speak peer-to-peer. |
| Scopists & proofreaders | Threatened | Acknowledge the concern honestly. Do not dismiss. |
| Privacy-first reporters | Firm opposition | Validate the concern. Explain the data model plainly. |

**Never speak down to any segment. The most skeptical person in the room is often the most experienced.**

## Brand Voice

See `references/brand-voice.md` for full detail. Core principles:

- **Empathetic first.** This profession has been told AI will kill their jobs for years. Earn trust before asking for attention.
- **Plain language.** Many reporters are not tech-savvy. No jargon, no buzzwords, no "AI-powered" chest-thumping.
- **Confident, not arrogant.** The product is good. State it plainly. Don't oversell.
- **Human.** Court reporting is a human profession. The brand sounds like a person, not a startup.
- **Accurate.** Never make a claim that isn't 100% true. This audience will fact-check you.

Tone words: **steady, warm, professional, honest, practical**
Avoid: hype, urgency bait, fearmongering, AI triumphalism, condescension

## Platform Rules

| Platform | Primary Use | Tone Adjustment |
|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn | Professional credibility, industry news | More formal. Speak to agency owners and senior reporters. |
| Facebook | Community, word of mouth | Warmest tone. Think Facebook Groups for court reporters. |
| Instagram | Visual storytelling, behind-the-scenes | Short captions. Show the product working. |
| X / Twitter | Quick takes, industry conversation | Punchy. Engage threads. Don't start fights. |
| TikTok | Education, younger reporters | Conversational. Tutorial-style. No corporate polish. |

## Content Pillars

1. **Product proof** — screenshots, error catches, before/after transcripts (anonymized)
2. **Reporter stories** — testimonials, day-in-the-life, what their workflow looks like
3. **Education** — tips for cleaner transcripts, common steno errors, industry facts
4. **Transparency** — how the product works, what data is used, privacy answers
5. **Community** — industry news, events (NCRA conference, state associations), shoutouts

## Comment Response Playbook

See `references/comment-responses.md` for detailed scripts. Quick guide:

- **Praise:** Thank sincerely. One sentence. Don't over-gush.
- **Curiosity / questions:** Answer directly. If it needs a longer answer, offer to DM.
- **Skepticism:** Validate the concern. Explain simply. Never argue.
- **Privacy concerns:** Take seriously. Explain data model (no storing of transcripts). Offer to follow up.
- **Job replacement fear:** Acknowledge it's a real fear with a real history. Reframe: tool for reporters, not a replacement for them.
- **Hostile / trolling:** One calm response maximum. Do not escalate. Do not delete unless abusive.
- **Scopists concerned about their livelihood:** Honest. Court Reportcard helps the reporter catch errors before it goes to a scopist, but scopists and proofers are still needed. Do not promise what isn't true.
