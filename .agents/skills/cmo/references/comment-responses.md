# Comment Response Playbook

## General Principles

- Respond within 24 hours when possible. Speed signals you're real.
- One voice, always. Every response sounds like the same person — warm, direct, professional.
- Never argue in public. One calm response, then offer to move to DM.
- Never delete critical comments unless they contain harassment, slurs, or private information.
- Do not use AI-sounding phrases: "Great question!", "Absolutely!", "Certainly!"

---

## Response Scripts by Type

### Praise / Positive Feedback

Keep it short and genuine. Don't over-gush.

> "This means a lot — thank you. Glad it's been useful on the job."
> "Really appreciate you sharing that. That's exactly what we built it for."
> "Thank you. Hope it keeps making your life easier."

If they tag someone or share a specific use case, acknowledge the specifics:
> "A [homophone catch / missing word flag] — that's exactly the kind of thing that's hardest to catch yourself after a long session. Glad it got it."

---

### Curiosity / Feature Questions

Answer directly. If complex, summarize and offer to DM.

> "Good question — [direct answer]. If you want more detail, feel free to DM us."
> "Right now [feature/behavior]. We're actively working on [related thing] — no timeline yet, but it's on the list."

Never promise a feature that doesn't exist or have a confirmed timeline.

---

### Skepticism ("Does this actually work?")

Validate the skepticism, then give them something real.

> "Fair to be skeptical — there's a lot of noise in this space. The best answer I can give is: try it. We'd rather you test it on a real transcript than take our word for it."
> "Skepticism is reasonable. We'd say: run it on a job you've already proofed yourself and see what it catches (or doesn't). That's the real test."

---

### Job Replacement Fear

This is the most important response to get right. Acknowledge the history, reframe clearly, don't dismiss.

> "That fear makes complete sense — this profession has been hearing 'AI will replace you' for years. Court Reportcard doesn't transcribe anything. It's a proofreading tool. The reporter is still the expert doing the work — we just flag the errors that are easy to miss at the end of a long day."
> "You've heard this before and you're right to be cautious. This isn't a transcription tool and it doesn't replace the reporter. It catches the mechanical errors — homophones, missing words, punctuation — so your work leaves the desk cleaner."

If they remain unconvinced: 
> "We hear you. No pressure. If that ever changes, we'll still be here."

---

### Privacy Concerns

Take it seriously. Be specific, not vague.

> "That's a legitimate concern — especially for anyone working sensitive cases. Here's how it works: [plain-language explanation of what is/isn't stored]. If you have more specific questions about how we handle data, DM us and we'll walk through it directly."

Never say "we take privacy seriously" alone. That phrase means nothing. Follow it with facts.

If you don't know the answer: 
> "That's a fair question and I want to give you an accurate answer rather than a vague one. Let me find out and get back to you — or DM us and we'll reply there."

---

### Scopist / Proofreader Concern

Honest, not defensive. Don't minimize.

> "That's a fair concern to raise. Court Reportcard catches the mechanical errors — homophones, missing words — before the transcript is finalized. It's not a replacement for a scopist. A cleaner draft going in also tends to make a scopist's review faster and less tedious. But we're not going to pretend it has zero impact on workflow — that wouldn't be honest."

---

### Criticism of Accuracy ("It missed something / it got something wrong")

Own it. Get specifics. Don't deflect.

> "That's not what we want to hear, and we appreciate you saying it rather than just walking away. Can you share more detail — what type of error, what context? We want to understand what happened."
> "That's a real problem and we want to fix it. DM us the details if you're comfortable — we take every miss seriously."

Never say "our AI made a mistake." Say: "we missed that" or "that's an error on our end."

---

### Hostile / Trolling

One calm response. Do not escalate. Do not match energy.

> "We hear you. If you ever want to have a real conversation about how it works, we're here."

Then stop. Do not reply again to that thread unless new substance is added.

---

### Competitors or Comparisons

Don't engage with competitor bashing. Acknowledge and redirect.

> "We can only speak to what we do — happy to walk you through that if it's helpful."

Never name-drop competitors. Never disparage them. The industry is small.

---

## Escalation Guide

| Situation | Action |
|---|---|
| Harassment / slurs | Delete and block |
| Real legal question about their specific case | "We can't give legal advice — please consult your attorney or association." |
| Press inquiry | DM to get contact info. Do not answer press questions in public comments. |
| Complaint that seems like potential lawsuit territory | Screenshot, do not respond publicly, escalate internally |
| Data breach claim or suggestion | Take offline immediately. Do not confirm or deny publicly. |
